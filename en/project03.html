Simulation of Particle Overlap

1. Introduction

In my first project, I studied mathematical criteria for the overlap of superellipses. These criteria are required for Monte Carlo simulations in statistical physics to avoid particle overlaps.

For spherical particles, calculating overlap is relatively simple. However, for elliptical and superelliptical particles, their orientation must also be considered. To determine the overlap of elliptical particles, the Vielliard-Baron criterion can be used. In this project, the focus was on superellipses, which are parameterized by the following equation:

\( \left| \frac{x}{a} \right| ^z + \left| \frac{y}{a} \right| ^z = 1 \)

Here, \(z\) is a rational number. In this project, the specific case \(z = 4\) was examined, and a numerical criterion for overlap was implemented.

2. Methods and Tools

The following tools were used to implement the project:

  • Mathematica: For the symbolic derivation and simplification of the overlap criterion.
  • Fortran: For the efficient implementation of the overlap criterion.

3. Implementation and Results

The approach to determining overlap is based on calculating the so-called Sylvester matrix and its resultant. The equations of two superellipses are used to form a Sylvester matrix. The resultant of the matrix becomes zero if the underlying equations of the superellipses share common roots, indicating an overlap of the superellipses.

To check for overlap, the Sylvester matrix must be numerically evaluated for different positions. In cases where the resultant changes its sign, an overlap is present.
