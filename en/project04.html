Simulation of Light Control by Two Dielectric Particles Near a Waveguide

1. Introduction

In my bachelor's thesis, I focused on simulating light propagation in optical waveguides. The goal was to develop methods to influence the direction of light propagation using nanoparticles.

2. Summary

Experimentally, it is already possible to place nanoparticles on or near an optical waveguide. In my work, I investigated how light propagation can be influenced solely through constructive and destructive interference of waves scattered by the nanoparticles.

To achieve this, I conducted numerical simulations on a system consisting of an optical waveguide and two dielectric particles placed near the surface of the waveguide. When light propagates through a thin waveguide, part of the wave emerges as an evanescent wave and is scattered by the particles.

3. Methods and Tools

The following tools were used for numerical simulations and analyses:

  • Python: To control numerical computations.
  • Pandas: To store and manage simulation results.
  • Netgen/NGSolve: For numerically solving the Helmholtz equations and simulating light propagation.

4. Results and Insights

The results show that it is possible to effectively control the direction of light propagation by strategically placing the particles and utilizing interference patterns. For a system with two scattering centers, it was demonstrated that the position and size of the particles significantly affect the interference pattern and, consequently, the light propagation.
