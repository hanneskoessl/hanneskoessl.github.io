Analysis of Measurement Data from a Particle Accelerator

1. Introduction

In this post, I want to provide an insight into my master's thesis, which I completed at the Institute of High Energy Physics of the Austrian Academy of Sciences. In addition to my results, I will also discuss the methods and tools I used.

2. Summary

The Standard Model of particle physics describes the weak, electromagnetic, and strong interactions between elementary particles. In the Standard Model, the strength of transitions between the three flavor generations of quarks in an interaction with a W boson is given by the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix (CKM matrix). This matrix is essential for understanding CP violation.

The goal of my thesis was to determine the matrix element |Vcb|, a key parameter of the CKM matrix that must be determined experimentally. In this work, I analyzed the exclusive semileptonic decay B0 → D*−ℓ+νℓ and data from the Belle experiment. For the analysis, I used the CLN parametrization (Caprini-Lellouch-Neubert parametrization), a mathematical model for describing semileptonic decays of heavy mesons.

3. Methods and Tools

For the analysis, I used various tools and frameworks:

  • Python: Main programming language for data preparation, analysis, and visualization.
  • NumPy: For implementing functions that model particle decay.
  • SciPy: For numerical computation of integrals.
  • Matplotlib: For creating visualizations and charts to present the results.

4. Results and Insights

The results of my analysis showed that the fit parameters of the CLN parametrization are stable, even under different assumptions and conditions. The following parameters were determined:

  • ρ2 = 1.166 ± 0.034
  • R1(1) = 1.184 ± 0.028
  • R2(1) = 0.848 ± 0.022
  • F(1)|Vcb|ηEW = (36.49 ± 0.46) × 10−3

These results contribute to better understanding existing discrepancies between different measurement methods and improving the precision of the CKM matrix modeling.
