Physical Analysis of Data from the Belle Experiment

1. Introduction

In this project, I analyzed the branching ratio of the B+ meson for the decay \( B^+ \rightarrow J/\psi(1S)K^+ \). For this, I evaluated data from the Belle experiment. The Belle experiment was an international project at the High Energy Accelerator Research Organization (KEK) in Tsukuba, Japan, which ran from 1999 to 2010.

The decay was reconstructed using the two sub-decays \( B^+ \rightarrow J/\psi(1S)K^+ \) and \( J/\psi(1S) \rightarrow e^+e^- \).

2. Methods and Tools

The following technologies were used for analysis and computation:

  • C++: To filter the data and search for specific particle decays.
  • CERN ROOT: For the statistical evaluation of the measurement data.

The signal selection was optimized using the “Figure of Merit”. A maximum-likelihood fit was applied first to Monte Carlo data and then to real data to determine the signal events.

3. Implementation and Results

The calculation of the branching ratio is based on the number of signal events, the total number of B events, the decay rates of the sub-decays, and the efficiency. The process is structured as follows:

  • Reconstruction of the decay using the sub-decays.
  • Optimization of signal selection criteria.
  • Application of the maximum-likelihood fit to Monte Carlo and real data.
  • Calculation of the branching fraction using efficiency and signal data.
